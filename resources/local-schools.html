What Schools Are Available in North Brisbane?

A comprehensive overview of primary and secondary schools in North Brisbane and Moreton Bay, including public, private, and specialized educational options.

Public Schools

Queensland public schools provide free education to all eligible students and are the most common choice for families in North Brisbane and Moreton Bay. These schools follow the Australian Curriculum and offer a comprehensive education with additional support services.

Primary Schools (Prep to Year 6)

Local primary schools serving different suburbs:

  • Albany Creek State School: Large school with extensive facilities, strong community focus
  • Arana Hills State School: Known for environmental education and outdoor learning
  • Aspley State School: Central location with diverse student population
  • Bracken Ridge State School: Strong performing arts program
  • Bridgemill State School: Smaller school with personalized attention
  • Burpengary State School: Growing school with modern facilities
  • Caboolture State School: Historic school with strong academic reputation
  • Camp Hill State Infants and Primary School: Focus on early childhood development
  • Carina State School: Multicultural community with inclusive programs
  • Clontarf Beach State School: Coastal location with unique environment

Secondary Schools (Year 7-12)

  • Albany Creek State High School: Large comprehensive high school with diverse programs
  • Aspley State High School: Strong academic results and extracurricular activities
  • Bracken Ridge State High School: Focus on vocational education and trades
  • Caboolture State High School: Large school with extensive facilities
  • Kallangur State High School: Known for sports excellence programs
  • Margate State High School: Smaller community-focused school
  • Redcliffe State High School: Coastal location with maritime programs
  • Sandgate State High School: Historic school with strong traditions

P-12 Schools

  • Eagle Junction State School: Combined primary and secondary, seamless transition
  • North Lakes State College: Large P-12 school with modern facilities
  • Redcliffe State High School (with feeder primaries): Connected primary-secondary pathways

Public School Features

  • Free education: No tuition fees for Queensland residents
  • Catchment areas: Priority enrollment for local residents
  • Diverse programs: Academic, vocational, and special education support
  • Extracurricular activities: Sports, arts, and clubs
  • Support services: Counseling, special education, and welfare support

Private and Independent Schools

Private schools offer alternative educational approaches with smaller class sizes and specialized programs. They charge tuition fees but provide additional resources and facilities.

Catholic Schools

  • St Peter's Primary School (Albany Creek): Strong academic focus with faith-based education
  • St John Fisher College: Co-educational secondary school with excellent facilities
  • St Rita's College (Clayfield): Girls' secondary school with strong traditions
  • St Paul's School (Bald Hills): Co-educational P-12 with modern campus
  • Padua College (Bracken Ridge): Boys' secondary school with strong sporting culture

Other Independent Schools

  • Morayfield State High School: Public school with independent-style programs
  • Westside Christian College: Christian-based education with strong community
  • Caloundra Christian College: Faith-based school with coastal location
  • The Gap State High School: Selective entry public school
  • Brisbane State High School: Selective academic public school

Private School Considerations

  • Tuition fees: Range from $3,000-$15,000+ per year
  • Class sizes: Typically smaller than public schools (15-20 students)
  • Specialized programs: IB, STEM, arts, or sports focuses
  • Facilities: Often include pools, gyms, and specialist equipment
  • Selection criteria: Academic results, interviews, or entrance exams

Specialized and Alternative Schools

Special Education Schools

Schools for students with additional learning needs:

  • Karana Downs School: Support for students with intellectual disabilities
  • Redbank Plains State High School (special education): Programs for diverse learning needs
  • Specialist units: Within mainstream schools for specific disabilities
  • Distance education: Queensland School of Distance Education for home-based learning

Schools with Special Programs

  • Queensland Academy for Science, Mathematics and Technology: Selective entry for gifted students
  • Brisbane State High School: Academic selective entry
  • Sports schools: Programs for talented athletes
  • Arts-focused schools: Music, drama, or visual arts specialization
  • Language immersion: Bilingual or international programs

Alternative Education Options

  • Flexible learning programs: For students needing different pacing
  • Community-based education: Local programs for disengaged students
  • Indigenous education centers: Culturally appropriate learning
  • Environmental schools: Outdoor and sustainability-focused education
  • Montessori schools: Child-led learning approaches

Choosing a School

Key Factors to Consider

Selecting the right school involves multiple considerations:

  • Location and transport: Distance from home and access to public transport
  • Catchment areas: Priority enrollment zones for public schools
  • Academic programs: Curriculum offerings and teaching approaches
  • School culture: Values, discipline policies, and community feel
  • Facilities and resources: Classrooms, libraries, sports facilities
  • Student support: Counseling, special education, and welfare services
  • Extracurricular activities: Sports, arts, and clubs available
  • Parent involvement: Opportunities for family participation

Research Process

  • School websites: Review programs, policies, and contact information
  • Open days: Visit schools during advertised open times
  • Parent forums: Local Facebook groups and community discussions
  • My School website: NAPLAN results and school performance data
  • School reviews: Independent feedback from current families
  • Principal meetings: Discuss philosophy and approach with leadership

Enrollment Processes

Public Schools

  • Catchment priority: Local residents get first preference
  • Out-of-catchment: Application process for non-local students
  • Prep enrollment: Birth date cut-offs and sibling priority
  • Year 7 intake: Automatic transition from feeder primary schools
  • Special programs: Auditions or applications for selective streams

Private Schools

  • Application forms: Detailed enrollment paperwork
  • Entrance assessments: Academic testing or interviews
  • Waiting lists: Popular schools may have long queues
  • Financial assistance: Scholarships or payment plans available
  • Trial periods: Some schools offer assessment periods

School Transitions

Starting Primary School

Transitioning from preschool to primary school:

  • Prep programs: Orientation visits and familiarization activities
  • Kindergarten visits: Regular attendance before starting
  • Parent information sessions: Understanding school expectations
  • Buddy systems: Older students helping new starters
  • Flexible start: Gradual introduction to full school days

Moving to Secondary School

  • High school orientation: Visits and information sessions
  • Subject selection: Choosing electives and pathways
  • Campus familiarization: Learning building locations and routines
  • Peer support programs: Year 7 buddies and mentors
  • Counselor guidance: Academic and personal support planning

Changing Schools

  • Transfer processes: Applications and enrollment procedures
  • Academic records: Transferring report cards and assessments
  • Social transition: Building new friendships and routines
  • Curriculum alignment: Ensuring continuity of learning
  • Emotional support: Counseling for adjustment difficulties

School Support Services

Academic Support

  • Learning support teachers: Additional help for struggling students
  • Gifted programs: Extension activities for advanced learners
  • Tutoring services: Before/after school academic help
  • Homework clubs: Supervised study sessions
  • Study skills programs: Organization and learning strategies

Welfare and Counseling

  • School counselors: Personal and academic guidance
  • Youth workers: Support for behavioral or social issues
  • Chaplains: Spiritual and emotional support
  • Peer support programs: Student-led mentoring
  • Crisis intervention: Emergency support services

Special Education Services

  • Individual education plans: Personalized learning programs
  • Teacher aides: Additional classroom support
  • Specialist teachers: Speech, occupational, or physical therapy
  • Assistive technology: Equipment and software support
  • Inclusive practices: Mainstreaming with appropriate support

School Performance and Accountability

My School Website

The Australian government's My School website provides:

  • NAPLAN results: School performance data
  • Student background information: Socio-economic indicators
  • School finances: Funding and expenditure details
  • Parent opinion surveys: Community feedback
  • School profiles: Programs and facilities information

Understanding School Data

  • ICSEA values: Index of Community Socio-Educational Advantage
  • Progress measures: Improvement over time
  • Equity analysis: Performance across different student groups
  • Comparative data: Similar schools' performance
  • Qualitative factors: Beyond test scores and statistics

School Improvement

  • Annual reports: School achievements and goals
  • Parent engagement: Involvement in school activities
  • Professional development: Teacher training and improvement
  • Community partnerships: Local business and organization support
  • Continuous review: Regular evaluation and adjustment

Future Planning

Education choices have long-term implications:

  • Pathways planning: University, TAFE, or vocational options
  • Subject selection: Building toward career goals
  • Extracurricular involvement: Developing well-rounded profiles
  • Leadership opportunities: Building confidence and skills
  • Transition support: Moving to further education or employment

Choosing a school is an important decision that should align with your child's needs, your family's values, and your educational goals. Take time to research options thoroughly and involve your child in the process where appropriate.
