What Parent Support Groups Are Available?

A comprehensive guide to parent networks, support groups, and community resources available for families in North Brisbane and Moreton Bay.

School Parent Associations

Parent associations play a vital role in connecting families with schools and enhancing the educational experience. These groups provide opportunities for parents to contribute to school life and support their children's education.

P&C Associations (Parents and Citizens)

Most Queensland state schools have active P&C associations:

  • Albany Creek State School P&C: Large, active association with diverse events
  • Aspley State High School P&C: Strong focus on student welfare and facilities
  • Caboolture State High School P&C: Community fundraising and support programs
  • Redcliffe State High School P&C: Coastal community with unique activities
  • North Lakes State College P&C: Growing association with modern school

What P&C Groups Do

  • Fundraising: School facilities, equipment, and programs
  • Events organization: Fetes, discos, and community gatherings
  • Parent education: Workshops on parenting and education topics
  • School advocacy: Voice for parents in school decision-making
  • Volunteer coordination: Classroom helpers and event support
  • Communication: Newsletters and information sharing

Getting Involved

  • Attend meetings: Usually held monthly in school terms
  • Join committees: Fundraising, events, or welfare sub-committees
  • Volunteer: Help with school events or classroom assistance
  • Contact school office: Express interest in participating
  • Start small: Begin with attending events before committing to roles

Online Communities

Local Facebook Groups

Digital communities provide immediate support and information sharing:

  • North Brisbane Parents: General parenting and education discussions
  • Albany Creek Community: Local news, events, and parent support
  • Aspley Parents Group: School-specific and general parenting advice
  • Caboolture Parents Network: Regional parenting community
  • Redcliffe Peninsula Parents: Coastal area family support
  • North Lakes Families: New suburb with growing community

Forum Benefits

  • Real-time advice: Immediate responses to questions
  • Local recommendations: School, service, and activity suggestions
  • Event sharing: Community activities and school information
  • Support network: Emotional support and shared experiences
  • Resource sharing: Hand-me-downs, services, and opportunities
  • Buy/sell groups: School uniforms and equipment

Online Safety and Etiquette

  • Privacy protection: Be cautious about sharing personal information
  • Respectful communication: Kind and supportive interactions
  • Fact-checking: Verify information before sharing
  • Group rules: Follow community guidelines
  • Positive focus: Emphasize solutions and support

Specialized Support Groups

For parents of children with specific learning needs:

  • Dyslexia Association Queensland: Support for reading difficulties
  • ADHD Queensland: Attention and hyperactivity support
  • Autism Queensland: Autism spectrum disorder resources
  • Learning Difficulties Australia: General learning support
  • Gifted and Talented Association: Support for advanced learners

Health and Disability Support

  • Down Syndrome Queensland: Family support and information
  • Cerebral Palsy League: Motor disability support
  • Spina Bifida Association: Neural tube defect support
  • Cystic Fibrosis Queensland: Chronic illness support
  • Parenting Children with Disabilities: General disability parenting

Cultural and Language Groups

  • Multicultural Communities Council: Settlement and cultural support
  • ESL Parent Groups: English language learning support
  • Indigenous Parent Networks: Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander support
  • Refugee Parent Groups: Newly arrived family support
  • Cultural Associations: Specific ethnic community groups

Mental Health Support

  • Beyond Blue Parents: Depression and anxiety support
  • Headspace Parent Groups: Youth mental health discussions
  • Anxiety Parents Network: Anxiety disorder support
  • COPE Parents: Center of Perinatal Excellence
  • Black Dog Institute: Mood disorder resources

Workshops and Educational Programs

Structured learning opportunities for parents:

  • Queensland Parenting Programs: Triple P and other evidence-based programs
  • School Readiness Workshops: Preparing children for school
  • Adolescent Development Sessions: Understanding teenage years
  • Digital Literacy for Parents: Online safety and technology
  • Financial Literacy: Managing education costs

Child Development Seminars

  • Early Childhood Development: Birth to school age
  • Learning Styles: Understanding how children learn
  • Emotional Intelligence: Building social skills
  • Nutrition and Health: Supporting physical development
  • Screen Time Management: Balancing technology use

Education System Workshops

  • NAPLAN Preparation: Understanding tests and supporting children
  • High School Transition: Moving from primary to secondary
  • Subject Selection: Choosing senior pathways
  • University Preparation: ATAR and tertiary options
  • Vocational Education: TAFE and apprenticeship pathways

Where to Find Workshops

  • Community centers: Local council-run programs
  • Schools: Parent education sessions
  • Libraries: Free community workshops
  • Health services: Parenting programs through hospitals
  • Universities: Extension programs and courses

Professional Parent Support Services

Counseling and Therapy

Professional support for parenting challenges:

  • Parenting helplines: 24/7 advice and support
  • Family counseling: Relationship and family therapy
  • Individual therapy: Personal mental health support
  • Couples counseling: Partnership support
  • Grief counseling: Loss and trauma support

Family Support Organizations

  • Relationships Australia: Family relationship counseling
  • Anglicare: Welfare and support services
  • Salvation Army: Emergency and family support
  • Red Cross: Disaster and crisis support
  • St Vincent de Paul: Financial and material aid

Legal and Advocacy Support

  • Legal Aid Queensland: Free legal advice
  • Tenants Queensland: Housing rights and support
  • Consumer protection: Education service complaints
  • Disability advocacy: Rights and accommodation support
  • Education advocacy: School and system navigation

  • Kindergarten playgroups: School-based preparation activities
  • Special needs playgroups: Inclusive early learning
  • Cultural playgroups: Language and cultural activities
  • Music playgroups: Musical development activities

Mother's Groups and New Parent Support

  • Breastfeeding groups: Feeding support and information
  • Parentcraft classes: Preparation for parenthood

Toddler Groups

Finding and Joining Groups

  • Local council websites: Community program directories
  • Library bulletin boards: Notices and flyers

  • Word of mouth: Recommendations from other parents

  • Moreton Bay Regional Council: Local program finder
  • Meetup.com: Interest-based parent groups
  • Facebook groups: Local parenting communities
  • Parenting websites: National support directories

Making Connections

  • Start small: Attend one meeting or event first
  • Be open: Share your experiences and listen to others
  • Follow through: Commit to groups that meet your needs
  • Give back: Volunteer and contribute to the community
  • Stay connected: Maintain relationships beyond initial meetings

Benefits of Parent Support

Connecting with other parents provides numerous advantages:

  • Emotional support: Sharing experiences reduces isolation
  • Practical advice: Solutions to common parenting challenges
  • Information sharing: School and service recommendations
  • Social connections: Building friendships and networks
  • Community involvement: Contributing to local education
  • Personal growth: Learning from others' experiences

Parent support groups create a sense of community and shared purpose. Whether seeking advice, offering help, or simply connecting with others who understand the joys and challenges of parenting, these groups provide valuable resources for families in North Brisbane and Moreton Bay.
